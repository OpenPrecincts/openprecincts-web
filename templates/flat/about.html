About Our Process

While our ultimate goal is to obtain high-quality districts for the entire nation, the path to getting there can vary quite a bit state to state. Here's a rough overview of how things work, but of course the real world is messy and you'll likely see that each state will have quirks all its own.

1

Research

First, we do a bit of research to determine the best path. This might mean we find that a state has a master map of precinct files, or it might mean that we find that a state delegates that to each individual county. Depending on how it looks, we'll initialize the state in question on this site to begin the process of collecting source data to cover the entire state.

2

Data Collection

Now we begin collecting precinct information, often at the county level. Of course sometimes this is easier, but the default fallback is to contact officials in each county and build up from there. In a best-case-scenario the state will provide us with a single file containing all precincts.

3

Validation & Publishing
